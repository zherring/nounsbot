This is a standing vote: an agent that evaluates every Nouns DAO proposal against a written constitution, and casts onchain with the clause that decided it — cited in the vote reason, every time.

Delegate your Noun — soon Read the constitution

Delegation is one transaction, holds no custody, and is reversible any time. The button ships once there's a voting record to judge us by.

Why this exists

Governance isn't bottlenecked on voting power — it's bottlenecked on attention. Every proposal runs its own ~9-day clock, and the cost of showing up to all of them is what keeps most Nouns dormant. Dormant supply is exactly what a captured minority needs to freeze the treasury. This project makes consistent participation nearly free: write your values down once, and the vote shows up every single time.

The constitution is the product

A versioned markdown document of governance values and decision rules. Every amendment is a public commit. The agent is fungible plumbing that executes it.

Human-ratified, machine-consistent

The agent proposes a verdict with clauses cited; a human ratifies or overrides — and every override requires a written reason, logged publicly.

Reasoning onchain

Votes are cast with the reasoning attached, visible in every Nouns client. No silent weight. The full verdict record is public and append-only.

How it works

1 · Watch

Event-driven ingest of every proposal and candidate. Calldata is decoded and checked against the prose — if the transactions don't match the claims, that's an automatic flag.

2 · Judge

Each proposal is evaluated against the constitution. Output: a vote, a confidence, and the specific clauses that drove it. Structural proposals always get human review.

3 · Vote

Cast onchain partway through the voting window — late enough to see the field, never late enough to miss it. Flip attempts in the final hours trigger alerts, not surprises.
